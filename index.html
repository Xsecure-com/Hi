Stealer Logs

                
hackers steal databases containing usernames, passwords, emails, and personal details from websites. these stolen databases, known as “logs” or “dumps,” are sold on the dark web. they are often used for credential stuffing, where hackers try the same login details on multiple sites since many people reuse passwords.

                
            

        


        

            

                
DDoS-For-Hire Services

                
distributed denial-of-service (ddos) attacks overwhelm a website or server with massive fake traffic, making it crash. hackers offer ddos-as-a-service, where anyone can pay to attack a website, often to sabotage competitors or blackmail businesses. these attacks are carried out using botnets (networks of hacked devices).

                
            

        


        

            

                
Fake Login Pages

                
hackers create exact copies of real websites like banks, social media, or crypto wallets. when a victim enters their login details, the hacker captures them. these pages are often spread through phishing emails, text messages, or ads. once stolen, credentials are used for account takeovers or sold on hacking forums.

                
            

        


        

            

                
Wi-Fi Hacking Tools

                
these tools break into wifi networks by cracking passwords or exploiting weak encryption. hackers use them to spy on internet traffic, steal passwords, or spread malware. public wifi networks are often targeted since many lack proper security, allowing hackers to intercept sensitive data like banking logins.

                
            

        


        

            

                
Session Hijacking Tools

                
hackers steal session cookies, which are small files that keep users logged into websites. by hijacking these cookies, they can access a victim’s account without needing a password. this method is often used on social media and email accounts, bypassing two-factor authentication (2fa) in many cases.

                
            

        


        

            

                
Credential Stealers

                
these programs extract saved usernames and passwords from web browsers, email clients, or apps. they run in the background and send stolen credentials to hackers. once obtained, these details are used for unauthorized access to bank accounts, social media, or even company systems.

                
            

        


        

            

                
RATs

                
a rat is a type of malware that allows hackers to fully control an infected device remotely. they can steal files, turn on the camera, log keystrokes, or even use the device for further attacks. rats are often spread through email attachments, pirated software, or fake updates.

                
            

        


        

            

                
ATM & POS Malware

                
hackers install malware on atms or point-of-sale (pos) machines to steal credit card information. when customers insert their cards, the malware captures their card number and pin. hackers use this data to create cloned cards or sell the details on the dark web.

                
            

        


        

            

                
Exploit Kits

                
these are automated tools that scan devices for known security flaws and exploit them to gain access. they are often used to spread malware or ransomware. many exploit kits work by targeting outdated software or unpatched systems, making regular software updates critical for security.

                
            

        


        

            

                
Cryptojacking Scripts

                
these scripts secretly run on a victim’s device and use its processing power to mine cryptocurrency for hackers. users experience slow devices, overheating, and high electricity bills. cryptojacking is often spread through infected websites or fake downloads.

                
            

        


        

            

                
Brute Force & Dictionary Attack Tools

                
these tools try thousands or millions of password combinations quickly to crack an account. brute force attacks test every possible combination, while dictionary attacks use common passwords from pre-made lists. weak or short passwords are easily cracked using these methods.

                
            

        


        

            

                
Man-In-The-Middle Tools

                
hackers intercept internet traffic between a victim and a website, allowing them to steal sensitive information like passwords or credit card details. mitm attacks are common on unsecured public wifi networks, where hackers can eavesdrop on users without them realizing it.

                
            

        


        

            

                
Keyloggers

                
keyloggers record everything a victim types on their keyboard, including passwords, messages, and credit card details. they are often installed through malware or usb devices. advanced keyloggers can also capture clipboard content and take screenshots.

                
            

        


        

            

                
Phishing Kits

                
these are pre-made tools for launching phishing attacks. they include fake email templates, cloned websites, and scripts for stealing login details. even beginner hackers can use these kits to trick victims into entering their credentials on fake websites.

                
            

        


        

            

                
Zero-Day Exploits

                
zero-days are software vulnerabilities that developers don’t yet know about, making them extremely dangerous. hackers use them to break into systems before a patch is released. these exploits are highly valuable and often sold on the dark web for huge sums.

                
            

        


        

            

                
Botnets

                
a botnet is a group of hacked devices controlled remotely by a hacker. botnets are used for ddos attacks, spreading malware, or mining cryptocurrency. victims often don’t realize their device is infected and being used in large-scale cyberattacks.

                
            

        


        

            

                
Packet Sniffers

                
these tools capture internet traffic and analyze it for sensitive data. hackers use them to steal login credentials, emails, and credit card details. packet sniffers are especially dangerous on public wifi networks, where data is often unencrypted.

                
            

        


        

            

                
Crypters & Packers

                
these tools hide malware from antivirus software by encrypting or modifying its code. hackers use them to ensure their viruses remain undetected. advanced crypters even change the malware’s appearance each time it runs, making detection harder.

                
            

        


        

            

                
Carding Tools & CVV Dumps

                
carding tools help hackers make fraudulent online purchases using stolen credit card details. cvv dumps contain credit card numbers, expiration dates, and security codes stolen from compromised payment systems. these details are sold in bulk on dark web marketplaces.
